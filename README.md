This project was developed for EasyVisa, a data science firm contracted by the Office of Foreign Labor Certification (OFLC) to address the growing challenge of processing US visa applications. With nearly 1.7 million positions reviewed in FY 2016 alone — a 9% increase from the previous year — the manual review process had become increasingly impractical. The goal was to build a machine learning solution capable of predicting whether a visa application should be certified or denied, while also uncovering the key factors that drive those decisions.
The dataset consisted of 25,480 visa applications containing information about both the applicant and their prospective employer, including education level, job experience, prevailing wage, region of employment, continent of origin, company size, and wage type. After cleaning the data — which included correcting negative values in the employee count column — exploratory analysis revealed that 66.8% of applications in the dataset were certified. The majority of applicants came from Asia (66.2%), with most targeting employment in the Northeast and South regions of the US. Higher education levels, prior job experience, and yearly salary structures were all strongly associated with higher certification rates.
Several classification models were built and evaluated, including Decision Trees, Bagging, Random Forest, AdaBoost, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, and a Stacking Classifier. All models were tuned using GridSearchCV with 5-fold cross-validation, and F1-Score was used as the primary evaluation metric to balance false positives and false negatives — both of which carry real consequences in the visa approval context. The Stacking Classifier, which combined AdaBoost, Gradient Boosting, and Random Forest with XGBoost as the meta-learner, delivered the best overall performance with a test accuracy of 74.5% and an F1-score of 82.1%.
Feature importance analysis identified prevailing wage, job experience, and education level as the strongest predictors of visa certification. Applicants most likely to be certified are those with at least a Bachelor's degree, prior work experience, applying for full-time yearly-salaried roles, and targeting employment in the Midwest or South regions of the US.
